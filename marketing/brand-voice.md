# UpSpeech Brand Voice

Shared across Instagram, LinkedIn, and any other channel. Platform-specific
framing lives in each platform's `strategy.md`; the voice below is constant.

## The three words

UpSpeech is **Calm, Scientific, Encouraging.**

- **Calm before all else.** No hype, no urgency, no shouting. The reader is
  often anxious about speaking. Lower the temperature.
- **Scientific.** Plain, accurate, credible. We respect the field and the
  clinician. We never overstate what the product does.
- **Encouraging.** Warm and on the reader's side. Progress, not pressure. We
  celebrate showing up, not just outcomes.

Design principles that carry into copy: *Progress not pressure. Clinical
credibility. Accessible by default. Personality through polish.*

## How it sounds

- Short, clear sentences. Speakable out loud.
- Concrete practice language: practise, warm up, slow down, log it, try the
  scenario, review with your therapist.
- Second person for patients ("your week", "you showed up"). Peer-to-peer for
  clinicians ("your notes", "your caseload").
- Personality through small, human lines, never through jokes at anyone's
  expense.

Good:

- "Five calm minutes, wherever you are."
- "You showed up again. That counts."
- "Arrive at your session with a week of progress."
- "Notes written while you focus on the person." (clinician)
- "Speaking up matters more than speaking smoothly."

Avoid:

- "Cure your stutter." "Speak fluently forever." "Fix your speech."
- "Sound normal." "Stop stuttering today."
- Anything that frames stuttering as a flaw, a weakness, or nerves.

## The sensitivity rules (hard requirements)

UpSpeech operates in a clinical, semi-regulated, sensitive space. These are not
style preferences. Breaking them is a brand and trust failure.

1. **No clinical claims or outcome promises.** No cures, no guarantees, no
   "results in X weeks", no implied diagnosis. The product supports therapy; it
   does not replace a clinician or promise a result.
2. **Fluency is not the goal.** Never imply the point is to stop stuttering or
   to sound fluent. The goal is confidence, communication, and tools. This is a
   stutter-positive brand.
3. **Never mock or imitate stuttering.** No repeated-letter "j-j-jokes", no
   playing a stutter for effect, ever.
4. **Person-first and identity-first, used with care.** "People who stutter"
   and "person who stutters" are both accepted by the community; use them. Avoid
   "stutterer" as a label and avoid "suffers from".
5. **Real people require written consent.** No real patient, real session, real
   clinic name, or real client data in any asset without documented consent.
   Generated people must not resemble a real, identifiable person.
6. **No medical or fear-based marketing.** No "is your child falling behind?"
   anxiety hooks aimed at parents.
7. **Accessibility is part of the brand.** High contrast, real alt text on
   every post, captions on every video. Accessible by default.

## Audience framing in one line each

- **People who stutter (teens/adults):** a calm, encouraging place to keep
  practising between sessions, with progress you can see.
- **Parents/caregivers:** reassurance and a clear path, never pressure or fear.
- **Speech-language pathologists:** less time on paperwork, more time with the
  person; a tool that respects clinical judgment.
- **The stuttering / neurodiversity community:** stutter-positive education that
  is worth saving and sharing, with nothing to sell in the post itself.

## Color and type (for any copy rendered as an image)

- Navy `#293587` primary. Accents: sky `#3b82f6`, mint/celebrate `#10b981`,
  gold `#f59e0b`, warm `#f97316`, lavender. Never raw off-palette hex.
- Outfit (headings), Plus Jakarta Sans (body), Bricolage Grotesque (accent).
- No em-dashes in copy. Use commas, periods, or parentheses.
