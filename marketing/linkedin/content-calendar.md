# LinkedIn Content Calendar

First four weeks. LinkedIn is text-first; drafts below are close to postable.
Keep them peer-to-peer, specific, and within the sensitivity rules in
[`../brand-voice.md`](../brand-voice.md). 2 to 3 posts per week, Tue-Thu mornings.

## Week 1

- **Time pillar (text)**
  > A speech-language pathologist told us she spends about an hour a day writing
  > session notes. Not seeing clients. Not planning therapy. Typing up what just
  > happened.
  >
  > That's the part we pointed UpSpeech at first. It drafts the clinical session
  > report from the session, and the clinician edits and approves it. The
  > judgment stays human. The typing mostly doesn't.
  >
  > What would you do with an extra five hours a week?

- **Credibility pillar (text)**
  > Stutter-positive isn't a tagline for us, it's a design constraint.
  >
  > UpSpeech never frames the goal as "stop stuttering." It supports the
  > technique a clinician sets, tracks practice, and builds confidence. The
  > clinician decides what progress means. The software just makes the week
  > between sessions visible.

## Week 2

- **Adherence pillar (document carousel)** — "Why between-session practice
  rarely happens (and what changes it)"
  > Cover: The homework problem every SLP knows.
  > Slides: 1) practice is invisible until the next session, 2) no feedback loop,
  > 3) no structure between visits, 4) what therapist-linked, visible practice
  > changes, 5) how UpSpeech does it. Save this if it sounds familiar.

- **Build-in-public (text)**
  > A small thing we got wrong early: we built progress charts for clinicians
  > first. Turned out the person who needed to *see* progress most was the
  > patient. Showing someone their own streak did more for adherence than any
  > dashboard we shipped for the clinic. Build for the person doing the work.

## Week 3

- **Product proof (graphic + text)** — quote/stat card via the template engine
  > Draft, edit, approve. The session note that used to take the evening now
  > starts as a draft you can sign off in a couple of minutes. (Sanitized
  > preview attached.) Documentation shouldn't be the reason clinicians burn out.

- **Time pillar (text)**
  > The pitch for clinical AI is usually "do more." Ours is quieter: do the same
  > clinical work, write less of it down by hand. The report drafting in UpSpeech
  > exists so the session can be about the person in the room.

## Week 4

- **Field moment (text)** — tie to a real awareness date when one lands
  > A reminder worth repeating in our field: stuttering is neurological, not a
  > sign of nerves or low intelligence. The people who stutter we build with are
  > articulate, capable, and tired of being told to "just slow down." We build
  > tools, not corrections.

- **CTA / pilot (text)**
  > We're working with a small number of clinics to refine UpSpeech for real
  > caseloads: AI report drafting plus a between-session practice app built for
  > the technique you set. If you're an SLP curious about a pilot, comment or DM.
  > Happy to show you the report flow first.

## Notes

- Lead with the observation, not the product. Mention UpSpeech once, in context.
- Document carousels and "I got this wrong" posts tend to earn the most saves
  and comments from SLPs.
- Reframe, never repost, the emotional Instagram pieces. Same truth, clinician
  lens.
- Reply to every comment from an SLP or clinic owner the day it lands.
