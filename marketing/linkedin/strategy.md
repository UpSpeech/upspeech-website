# UpSpeech LinkedIn Strategy

## Account role

LinkedIn is where UpSpeech earns clinical trust. The audience is professional:
speech-language pathologists, clinic owners, university clinics, and the
adjacent digital-health field. The job is to be credible and useful, not loud.

Where Instagram leads with the patient and the encouragement, LinkedIn leads
with the clinician's day: the documentation burden, between-session adherence,
and the evidence behind structured practice. Same product, different doorway.

## Positioning

**AI clinical documentation plus a between-session practice platform, built with
speech-language pathologists.**

Supporting messages:

- Draft the session report in seconds, then edit and approve. Less time on
  notes, more time with the person.
- Patients keep practising between sessions, on the technique you set, and you
  see the trends before the next appointment.
- Multi-tenant and built for real clinical workflows, not a consumer toy.
- Stutter-positive by design. The tool supports your judgment; it doesn't
  replace it or promise outcomes.

## Audiences

1. **Practising SLPs** (especially fluency/stuttering specialists). The core.
2. **Clinic owners and practice managers.** Care about throughput, adherence,
   documentation load, and staff time.
3. **University clinics and SLP students/educators.** Credibility and reach.
4. **Digital-health and founder peers.** Build-in-public, partnerships, hiring.

## Voice

Brand voice (`../brand-voice.md`) at the scientific, credible end. Peer-to-peer,
plain, specific. Cite the field honestly; never overstate. The sensitivity rules
hold: no clinical claims, no cures, no patient used as a sales prop.

## Content pillars

1. **Clinician time** — the documentation burden and how AI report drafting
   gives minutes back. The clearest, most-felt value.

2. **Between-session adherence** — the clinical problem that homework rarely
   gets done, and what structured, visible, therapist-linked practice changes.

3. **Clinical credibility** — how the product respects clinical judgment,
   stutter-positive framing, data handling, and the field's evidence base.

4. **Product proof** — real (sanitized) workflows: the report draft, the
   patient progress view, the therapist dashboard.

5. **Field and build-in-public** — stuttering-awareness moments (ISAD, Oct 22),
   honest founder notes, what shipped, what was learned.

## Formats

- **Text posts** (the staple): a specific observation, a short story from
  building with SLPs, or a plain explainer. 3 to 6 short paragraphs.
- **Document carousels** (PDF): "5 ways the session note eats your evening",
  "what between-session adherence actually requires". High saves.
- **Single graphics**: quote cards and one-stat cards via the template engine.
- **Founder/clinician video**: occasional, captioned, talking-head or screen
  recording. Not required to start.

## Cadence

- 2 to 3 posts per week. Quality and credibility over volume.
- Best windows: Tuesday to Thursday mornings.
- Roughly: one time/adherence post, one credibility or proof post, one
  field/build-in-public post per week.
- Engage in comments on SLP and digital-health posts; that is where reach comes
  from on LinkedIn.

## What "good" looks like

- Saves and thoughtful comments from actual SLPs.
- DMs from clinic owners asking about pilots.
- Reshares by SLP educators or fluency specialists.
- Profile-to-site clicks on the proof and time-savings posts.

## What we don't do here

- No fear-based or guilt-based hooks ("are your notes out of compliance?").
- No patient story without written consent and a clear point.
- No outcome or efficacy claim the product can't stand behind.
- No reposting Instagram's emotional posts verbatim; reframe for the clinician.
