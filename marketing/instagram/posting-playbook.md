# Instagram Posting Playbook

What to post, where, how often, and when to come back for the next batch.

## Quick verdict on the current assets

The starter set is a complete first launch system: a coherent 9-post grid, a
stutter-positive education frame, progress and companion visuals, photo-overlay
posts, a how-it-works carousel, prep-day stories, and six highlight covers. It
uses the product design system instead of generic clinical-software tropes.

The next quality jump comes from real product screenshots, real companion
animations, consented patient or community voices, and short screen recordings.

## What to post where

### Feed

Durable ideas that should greet anyone landing on the profile:

- Positioning: "Therapy that doesn't stop at the clinic door."
- Practice and progress proof: exercises, rings, streaks, technique scores.
- Stutter-positive education: myth-busting, affirming statements.
- The companion / encouragement personality posts.
- A few credible "for SLPs" posts (the deeper B2B push is on LinkedIn).
- Clear, low-pressure CTAs.

Use carousels when an idea needs steps:

- "What a week with UpSpeech looks like."
- "5 things people get wrong about stuttering."
- "From session to practice to progress."
- "Easy onset, explained in 4 slides."

### Stories

Timely, lighter, participatory:

- Daily practice checklists ("today's 5 minutes").
- Technique and scenario polls.
- Progress reveals (ring / streak).
- Stutter-positive fact drops (save-and-share).
- Question stickers ("word of the day", "where do you practise?").
- Short product demos and companion clips.

### Highlights

Permanent navigation for new visitors:

- `Practice`, `Progress`, `Techniques`, `For SLPs`, `Stories`, `Facts`.

### Reels

Once screen recordings or companion animations exist:

- A single guided rep, calm voiceover, captions on.
- Companion reaction loops.
- 15-second myth-busters.
- A "the note wrote itself" SLP demo.

Do not force reels with generic clinic stock footage. Real product motion and
genuinely useful practice tips earn more than mood video.

## Posting frequency

Sustainable starting cadence:

- 3 feed posts per week.
- 1 carousel per week.
- 2 to 4 story frames on practice days, polls, and awareness drops.
- 1 reel per week once recordings exist.
- Refresh highlights when a new evergreen story belongs there.

If time is tight, drop to 2 feed posts per week and let stories respond to real
activity. Consistency beats volume, especially with an anxious audience: a calm,
reliable cadence is itself on-brand.

## First 3 weeks

See `content-calendar.md` for the full mapping. In short:

- **Week 1:** manifesto, stutter-positive, practice + streak story.
- **Week 2:** progress, the how-it-works carousel, companion + polls.
- **Week 3:** learning path, scenarios, product mock + myth story.

## Caption pattern

1. Strong, calm first line.
2. One real moment the reader recognizes.
3. How UpSpeech helps, plainly.
4. A practice or session takeaway.
5. One clear, low-pressure CTA.

## When to come ask for more posts

Come back every 2 weeks while launching, before scheduled posts run out.

- Every 2 weeks: ask for 6 feed posts, 2 carousels, 4 stories, 1 reel script.
- Monthly: a performance review and a refreshed calendar.
- When a feature ships: updated templates and a launch post.
- When real, shareable usage data exists: proof-led posts using those numbers.
- Before International Stuttering Awareness Day (Oct 22) or a clinic
  partnership launch: a campaign batch.

Lead time: at least 5 days for a normal batch, 10 to 14 days for a campaign.

## What to send for the next batch

- Which posts performed best and worst.
- Real screenshots, companion animations, or consented voices to feature.
- New features shipped since the last batch.
- Any metrics you're allowed to share.
- Audience replies, objections, and FAQs.
- The next goal: signups, awareness reach, SLP trust, or feature education.

Useful prompt:

```text
Create the next 2-week UpSpeech Instagram batch.

Goal: [signups / awareness / SLP trust / feature education]
Audience focus: [people who stutter / parents / community / SLPs]
What worked: [top posts]
What changed in product: [new features / screenshots]
Need: 6 feed posts, 2 carousels, 4 stories, 1 reel script, captions, exports.
```

## Quality bar before posting

- Read the post against `../brand-voice.md`. No clinical claims, no
  fluency-as-goal, no mocking, no fear hooks.
- Check text at phone size and thumbnail size.
- One clear idea per post; the CTA is specific and low-pressure.
- Replace CSS product mocks with real sanitized screenshots when available.
- Write real alt text. Add captions to every video.
- Don't run multiple sales-y CTA posts back to back.
- Save education posts into the `Facts` and `Techniques` highlights.

## Practical publishing notes

- Pin the manifesto, one progress/proof post, and one CTA post.
- Bio states it plainly: "Speech therapy that keeps going between sessions.
  Stutter-positive. Built with SLPs."
- One link destination at first: signup or the "ask your therapist" page.
- Keep hashtags specific and minimal (5 to 8).
- Reply to every early comment and DM. With this audience, trust is the metric.
