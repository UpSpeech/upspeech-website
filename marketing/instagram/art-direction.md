# Instagram Art Direction

## Direction: Calm Practice Studio

The social system should feel like a calm, modern clinic-adjacent studio:
soft surfaces, rounded cards, gentle progress visuals, and a friendly companion
character. Confident and credible, never sterile; encouraging, never childish.
It extends the product's "Soft Clinical Modern" aesthetic into something more
editorial and memorable than the app UI.

Avoid clinical-software tropes (stethoscopes, waveforms-as-decoration, sterile
blue gradients), generic SaaS hero gradients, stock "person on a headset"
imagery, and anything that reads as anxious or medical.

## Design system anchors

Pull directly from the product:

- Navy `#293587` (primary). The brand's anchor.
- Sky `#3b82f6` (info), mint/celebrate `#10b981`, gold `#f59e0b`, warm
  `#f97316`, lavender. Accents as signals, not full backgrounds.
- Soft light background (`#f3f5fd`), white paper cards, deep-navy ink.
- Outfit (headings, 700-800, tight tracking), Plus Jakarta Sans (body),
  Bricolage Grotesque (kickers and labels, uppercase).
- Product cues: progress rings, streak dots, technique scores, learning-path
  steps, scenario cards, the companion character, session reports.

## Social additions

More warmth and personality than the app UI:

- Large Outfit headlines, calm and confident.
- The companion character as a recurring, friendly mark (rounded face, gentle
  smile, mint "spark"). It is the personality-through-polish layer.
- Progress rings and streaks used as hero visuals, not just data.
- Soft dot-grid texture and rounded cards with low, soft shadows.
- Navy statement frames for the stutter-positive education posts.
- Human, encouraging copy that sounds like it is on the reader's side.

## Layout rules

- Readable at profile-thumbnail size. One strong idea per square.
- Keep critical copy clear of the outer 80px safe edge.
- Large type, short copy. Push explanation to captions or carousel slides.
- Strong contrast everywhere (accessibility is part of the brand).
- Never let the grid feel like nine identical cards. Alternate light posts,
  the navy statement frame, the companion post, and progress visuals.
- Use real product screenshots where they exist. Do not generate fake UI; use
  the CSS product mocks (`post-mock`) as honest, stylized stand-ins until real
  screenshots are sanitized.

## Template families

1. **Manifesto** — big claim, supporting line, companion, warm soft ground.
2. **Practice / Feature** — one capability shown through a concrete habit
   (exercise list, technique, scenario grid).
3. **Progress** — rings, streaks, weekly recap, technique scores.
4. **Companion / Encouragement** — the character plus affirming lines.
5. **Stutter-positive statement** — navy frame, one strong sentence, myth/fact.
6. **Story utility** — checklist, poll, progress reveal, fact drop, photo.

## Image generation use

Use AI image generation for:

- Calm desk and home surfaces with soft daylight (morning practice mood).
- A hand holding a phone mid-practice (screen left blank for compositing).
- Calm clinician desk scenes for the "for SLPs" posts.
- Abstract soft textures and campaign atmosphere.

Do not use generated images for:

- Exact product UI (use real screenshots or the CSS mocks).
- Final text, headlines, technique names, or numbers.
- Clinical claims or anything implying an outcome.
- Any identifiable real person who hasn't consented.

## Source-image prompt starters

Generate the scene first, place final text in templates afterward:

- "Calm morning desk near a window, a phone resting face-up with a blank dark
  screen, a glass of water and a small plant, soft natural light, editorial
  lifestyle photography, shallow depth of field, no readable text, no logos,
  no people."
- "Close warm photo of an adult's hand holding a modern phone with a blank dark
  screen, soft daylight, calm neutral background, leave the screen clean for
  later screenshot replacement, no readable text, no UI, no logos, no face."
- "Tidy speech-language clinician's desk, soft daylight, a notebook and a laptop
  with a blank dark screen, calm and professional, editorial lifestyle
  photography, no readable text, no logos, no identifiable people."
