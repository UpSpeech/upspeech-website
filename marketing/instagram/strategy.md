# UpSpeech Instagram Strategy

## Account role

The UpSpeech Instagram account is a calm, encouraging companion for people who
stutter and the people around them. It is not a feature-list ad account. The
feed should make practice feel approachable, make progress feel visible, and
make stuttering feel like something to work *with*, not hide.

The clinical, time-savings story for SLPs lives mostly on LinkedIn. On
Instagram, the clinician shows up as a quiet, credible presence (a few "for
SLPs" posts and the For SLPs highlight), not the lead message.

## Positioning

**Speech therapy that keeps going between sessions.**

Supporting messages:

- Guided daily practice shaped around the technique your therapist set.
- Progress you can actually see: streaks, technique scores, trends.
- Gentle encouragement built in, every day.
- Practise the hard conversations safely before the real moment.
- A stutter-positive brand: confidence and tools, never a demand to be fluent.

## Audiences

In priority order for this channel:

1. **People who stutter (teens and adults).** The core. Practice, progress,
   encouragement, scenario rehearsal.
2. **Parents and caregivers.** Reassurance and a clear path. Never fear-based.
3. **The stuttering and neurodiversity community.** Stutter-positive education
   that gets saved and shared. Builds goodwill and reach.
4. **Speech-language pathologists.** A credible, low-frequency presence; the
   real B2B push is on LinkedIn.

## Voice

See [`../brand-voice.md`](../brand-voice.md). On Instagram, lean into the
*encouraging* and *calm* notes most. Speak to one person. Celebrate showing up.

## Content pillars

1. **Practice** — the daily habit. Exercises, techniques, short reps,
   warm-ups, streaks. Make it feel like five calm minutes, not homework.

2. **Progress** — make improvement visible. Rings, streaks, technique trends,
   weekly recaps, before/after-a-week framing. The "Progress not pressure"
   pillar in visual form.

3. **Encouragement (Companion)** — the personality layer. The companion
   character, daily affirmations, "you showed up" moments. Warmth and polish.

4. **Stutter-positive education** — the awareness pillar. Myth-busting, facts,
   community-affirming statements. The most shareable content. Nothing to sell
   in the post itself.

5. **For clinicians** — credibility and the between-session story for SLPs.
   AI report writing, therapist-linked feedback, "your work continues at home."
   Low frequency on this channel.

## Cadence

- 3 feed posts per week.
- 1 carousel per week (often the awareness or how-it-works piece).
- 2 to 4 story frames on practice days, polls, and awareness drops.
- 1 reel per week once product screen recordings or companion animations exist.
- Pinned: the manifesto post, one progress/proof post, one CTA post.

## First 9-post grid

The first grid should read as a landing page for the whole idea:

1. Manifesto: "Therapy that doesn't stop at the clinic door."
2. Practice: "Practice that meets you where you are."
3. Progress: "Progress you can actually see."
4. Companion: "A little encouragement, every single day."
5. For clinicians: "Notes written while you focus on the person."
6. Stutter-positive: "Speaking up matters more than speaking smoothly."
7. Learning path: "One step at a time."
8. Scenarios: "Rehearse the hard conversations, safely."
9. CTA: "Speech therapy, supported all week."

These are built and exported in `templates/` as `feed-01` through `feed-09`.

## Story and highlight system

Highlights (covers built as `highlight-*`):

- `Practice`: daily exercises, techniques, warm-ups.
- `Progress`: streaks, scores, weekly recaps.
- `Techniques`: easy onset, pacing, light contact, explained simply.
- `For SLPs`: the clinician story, AI reports, between-session feedback.
- `Stories`: patient and community wins (consented only).
- `Facts`: stutter-positive myth-busting and education.

Story formats:

- Daily practice checklist ("today's 5 minutes").
- Technique or scenario poll ("which moment feels hardest?").
- Progress reveal (ring or streak).
- Myth/fact drop (stutter-positive, save-and-share).
- Question sticker ("where do you practise?", "word of the day").
- Photo overlay (calm desk, phone in hand) once source imagery exists.

## Hashtag direction

Small, specific, community-respectful. Rotate within these:

- #Stuttering #StutterPositive #PeopleWhoStutter #Stammering
- #SpeechTherapy #SLP #SLPeeps #SpeechLanguagePathology
- #Neurodiversity #ISAD (International Stuttering Awareness Day, Oct 22)

Avoid stigmatizing tags and avoid spammy 30-tag blocks. Five to eight is enough.

## Success metrics

- Saves and shares on Practice, Progress, and Facts posts.
- Replies to story prompts (word of the day, scenario polls).
- Profile clicks from awareness and progress posts.
- DMs from people who stutter and from parents (qualitative signal).
- Signup / "ask your therapist" link clicks once the link is live.
